## Conflicts, uncertainty, and inference

- The short PDF says “a decade of professional TypeScript experience”; the longer PDF’s dated history starts in 2015 and includes JavaScript roles before TypeScript. The rendered wording keeps “a decade of professional TypeScript experience” as the candidate’s claim and separately preserves the dated history.
- The short PDF says “AI since the first releases of Copilot and ChatGPT”; the longer PDF adds MidJourney. The selected profile uses the more defensible broad claim “AI product engineering” and does not repeat a release-timing claim.
- The short PDF labels Hunt’s product work “AttackCapture™ and HuntSQL™”; the longer PDF repeats those names. No product metrics or user counts are inferred.
- The PDFs use slightly different punctuation and “visualisations”/“visualisations”; the rendered copy uses the candidate’s British spelling and normalises arrows, dashes, and sentence case for readability.
- The longer PDF calls the candidate “self-guided missile” and includes an invitation to reach out. These are voice/context, not evidence, so they are omitted from the tight two-page version.
- The project names `11aiai.rj11.io` and `11benchbench.rj11.io` are rendered as links using those domain names because the PDFs present them as project URLs. If either domain is no longer live, the visible factual name remains unchanged.

## Compression choices

The two-page budget keeps the complete five-role recent experience, but each role is limited to three bullets. Earlier roles retain employer, title, dates, and one factual description. Detailed Glaiveware technology inventories, the full list of OMEGA configuration views, the full Phantasma internal-tool list, Legacy Github, and the longer personal narrative are omitted or compressed. No employer, date, credential, claim, or link was invented.
