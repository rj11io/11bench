# CV research

Access date for every web source: **2026-07-16**.

## Method and limits

This research was conducted for a senior AI product / frontend engineering
profile, not for a generic graduate resume. I prioritised current employer
guidance, university career services, ATS vendor documentation, peer-reviewed
research, accessibility standards, and primary design-system documentation.

Resume research is unusually full of repeated but weakly sourced claims. In
particular, the widely quoted “six- or seven-second scan” comes from a small,
commercial eye-tracking study. I use it as directional evidence for strong
hierarchy, not as a universal law. A larger peer-reviewed eye-tracking study
supports the broader point that work experience, education, clarity, and time
to absorb the document matter.

No external research was used to add facts about the candidate. The two PDFs in
`ref/` remain the only evidence for personal history and claims.

## Structure and senior-role evidence

### 1. Google — Resume-writing tips

- URL:
  <https://services.google.com/fh/files/misc/resume-writing-tips-for-veterans-2021.pdf>
- Finding: Google recommends a one- or two-page resume, an optional short impact
  summary, reverse chronology, visible employer/title/dates, consistent bullets,
  and accomplishment statements that explain evidence and method.
- Decision changed: Use two pages for a decade-long career; place industry
  experience before education; lead with a short positioning statement; write
  bullets around verified scope and contribution. No metric is added where the
  PDFs provide none.

### 2. OpenAI — Frontend Engineer, ChatGPT Engineering

- URL:
  <https://openai.com/careers/frontend-engineer-chatgpt-engineering-san-francisco/>
- Finding: A current AI-product frontend role emphasises trustworthy,
  high-quality user experiences, React/TypeScript, frontend architecture,
  testing, accessibility, reliability, and close product/design/research/backend
  collaboration.
- Decision changed: Present Ricardo as an end-to-end product engineer with a
  frontend systems foundation. Recent experience leads with products,
  architecture, data interfaces, quality tooling, and ambiguous zero-to-one
  work—not a detached framework inventory.

### 3. GitLab — Senior Frontend Engineer job description

- URL:
  <https://handbook.gitlab.com/job-description-library/engineering/development/frontend/senior/>
- Finding: Senior frontend evidence includes high-scope problem solving,
  technical and product influence, tested and performant delivery, component
  systems, collaboration, and enabling other engineers.
- Decision changed: Preserve evidence of first-hire ownership, team growth,
  onboarding, standards, monorepos, component libraries, testing, CI/CD, and
  technical leadership. Do not imply people management or mentorship beyond
  what the PDFs state.

## Scanning behaviour and information priority

### 4. Using Machine Learning with Eye-Tracking Data to Predict if a Recruiter Will Approve a Resume

- URL: <https://doi.org/10.3390/make5030038>
- Finding: This 2023 peer-reviewed study collected eye-tracking data from 221
  recruiters hiring computer-science majors. It reinforces work experience as a
  primary signal and concludes that resumes should be clear, concise, easy to
  absorb, and give particular attention to experience and education.
- Decision changed: Experience occupies most of both pages. Education is kept
  concise but explicit. Decorative material never displaces work evidence.

### 5. TheLadders — Keeping an eye on recruiter behavior

- URL:
  <https://www.theladders.com/static/images/basicSite/pdfs/TheLadders-EyeTracking-StudyC2.pdf?type=standard>
- Finding: The commercial study (30 recruiters; original research dated 2012,
  later circulated in an updated edition) found a very short initial scan and
  heavy attention to name, current and previous role/company/dates, and
  education. Clear hierarchy and reduced clutter improved rated usability.
- Decision changed: The top of page one exposes identity, title, location,
  professional links, positioning, and recent chronology immediately. Each role
  keeps title, company, and dates in a repeatable pattern. The exact scan-time
  number is not treated as universal.

### 6. Nielsen Norman Group — The Layer-Cake Pattern of Scanning Content on the Web

- URL: <https://www.nngroup.com/articles/layer-cake-pattern-scanning/>
- Finding: Descriptive headings and well-labelled chunks support efficient
  scanning. Users skip superfluous material and use headings to decide where to
  read more deeply.
- Decision changed: Use conventional labels—Profile, Experience, Earlier path,
  Open source, Technical range, Education—and clear role headings. The visual
  numbering is secondary; the words remain the information-bearing labels.

### 7. Indeed — How to read an applicant resume

- URL: <https://www.indeed.com/hire/c/info/how-to-read-a-resume>
- Finding: Employer guidance describes a fast first pass for relevant
  presentation, summary, skills, and keywords followed by deeper examination of
  role-relevant evidence.
- Decision changed: Design for progressive disclosure. Page one confirms fit;
  bullets provide the second-pass evidence. Key tools also appear in the roles
  where they were used, rather than only in the skills section.

## Editorial, typographic, and information-design direction

### 8. Nielsen Norman Group — Visual Hierarchy in UX

- URL: <https://www.nngroup.com/articles/visual-hierarchy-ux-definition/>
- Finding: Scale, contrast, grouping, whitespace, and proximity establish
  hierarchy. Too many competing colours or treatments flatten it.
- Decision changed: Use three dominant levels—name, section/role, body/meta—and
  one blue accent plus one small chartreuse identity marker on screen. Rules,
  spacing, and repeatable alignment do most of the work.

### 9. U.S. Web Design System — Typography

- URL: <https://designsystem.digital.gov/components/typography/>
- Finding: USWDS recommends flush-left type, a comfortable screen body size,
  45–90-character measures (about 66 for sustained text), appropriate leading,
  limited uppercase, and whitespace that groups a heading with its content.
- Decision changed: Keep paragraphs left aligned and narrow, reserve uppercase
  mono for short labels, avoid justified text, and use proximity rather than
  boxes to show relationships. Small CV metadata is treated as companion text,
  not the default reading size.

### 10. Figma — Web design trends for 2026

- URL: <https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/>
- Finding: Bold typography and exaggerated hierarchy remain a current way to
  establish character and a fast first impression.
- Decision changed: Give the screen version a confident, oversized name and
  typographic identity. Motion, layered type, and spectacle were rejected
  because they add no hiring signal and do not transfer to print.

### 11. Swiss Style forever — Swiss Federal Department of Foreign Affairs

- URL:
  <https://www.aboutswitzerland.eda.admin.ch/en/swiss-style-forever-the-story-of-a-graphic-design-tradition>
- Finding: Swiss graphic design is characterised by conceptual strength,
  concision, technical precision, grid systems, and typography used as the
  primary visual language.
- Decision changed: Use an asymmetric editorial grid, flush-left sans serif,
  monospaced metadata, precise rules, and restrained colour. The result should
  feel engineered and authored rather than templated or decorated with generic
  “AI” imagery.

### 12. Monotype — Human Types

- URL: <https://typetrends.monotype.com/human-types/>
- Finding: Monotype’s current type research argues for human judgement,
  cultural intent, and curation in response to generic machine-produced
  aesthetics.
- Decision changed: Avoid circuitry, glowing gradients, robot imagery, and
  synthetic visual clichés. Personality comes from editorial decisions and the
  candidate’s actual language and record.

## ATS and parsing

### 13. MIT Career Advising — Interphase 2026 resume resources

- URL:
  <https://capd.mit.edu/blog/2026/06/23/interphase-2026-resume-resources/>
- Finding: MIT advises relevance over volume, warns against shrinking body type
  or whitespace excessively, and recommends ATS testing. Its ATS guidance
  cautions against text boxes, tables, colours, and stylised fonts; it recommends
  supported file types and checking a plain-text conversion.
- Decision changed: Keep all content as selectable HTML text in a simple DOM
  order. The grid is CSS presentation, not a table or text-box construction.
  Print is validated by text extraction as well as by appearance.

### 14. Greenhouse — Unsuccessful resume parse

- URL:
  <https://support.greenhouse.io/hc/en-us/articles/200989175-Unsuccessful-resume-parse>
- Finding: Graphics, image-only documents, text boxes, complex tables,
  multi-column layouts, inconsistent headings, and contact details placed only
  in headers/footers can interfere with parsing.
- Decision changed: Use real headings, lists, anchors, and full factual titles.
  No portrait, chart, skill meter, icon-only contact control, or rasterised text
  is used. Important contact details appear in the main first-page header.

### 15. Lever — Understanding resume parsing

- URL:
  <https://help.lever.co/hc/en-us/articles/20087345054749-Understanding-resume-parsing>
- Finding: Lever accepts PDF and Word documents but not image files; selectable
  text is a useful basic test.
- Decision changed: The print PDF must retain selectable, extractable text and
  active links. Employer-specific upload instructions still take precedence
  because ATS behaviour is not identical across systems.

## Accessibility, links, and PDF

### 16. W3C — WCAG 2.2 guidance

- URLs:
  - <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reflow.html>
  - <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum>
  - <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/info-and-relationships.html>
  - <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/link-purpose-in-context.html>
  - <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/focus-visible.html>
- Finding: Content should reflow without two-dimensional scrolling at a narrow
  viewport; normal text needs at least 4.5:1 contrast; relationships must be
  programmatic as well as visual; links need understandable purpose; keyboard
  controls need visible focus.
- Decision changed: Mobile becomes a single stream at 375 px. Semantic headings,
  lists, landmarks, meaningful link text, a real print button, a skip link, and
  visible focus styles are used. Links remain underlined rather than relying on
  blue alone.

### 17. Adobe — Creating accessible PDFs

- URL:
  <https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/using/creating-accessible-pdfs.html>
- Finding: PDF accessibility depends on source structure, tags, logical reading
  order, headings, active links, and document language. Adobe recommends fixing
  source structure before conversion.
- Decision changed: Build a logical semantic HTML source and verify page order,
  link annotations, and extractable text. Browser-generated PDFs are not claimed
  to be formally PDF/UA conformant; `window.print()` cannot guarantee complete
  tagging across browsers.

### 18. MDN — `@page`, `break-after`, and `print-color-adjust`

- URLs:
  - <https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/At-rules/%40page>
  - <https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/break-after>
  - <https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/print-color-adjust>
- Finding: `@page` establishes physical page size and margins; `break-after`
  controls deliberate pagination; print colour adjustment is advisory and user
  settings can still override it.
- Decision changed: Define A4 explicitly, build exactly two page regions, break
  after page one only, hide screen controls, and reset every print surface to
  black on white. Page count and clipping must be verified in an actual browser,
  not inferred from CSS alone.

## Resulting design brief

- A calm “technical editorial dossier”: expressive at first glance, conventional
  when scanned, and free of generic AI decoration.
- Page one: identity, contacts, concise positioning, career signals, and the
  three most recent roles.
- Page two: remaining senior roles, a compressed complete earlier chronology,
  open-source work, technical range, education, and the robotics distinction.
- One semantic reading order; CSS creates the visual grid.
- Real text and real links throughout.
- Screen: responsive at 375 px and deliberate at 1440 px.
- Print: A4 at 100%, two explicit pages, black on white, no controls, no clipped
  role, no blank third page, selectable text, and active links.
