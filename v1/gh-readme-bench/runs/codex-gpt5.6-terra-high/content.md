## Editorial selection

### Keep in the main path

1. Identity and one-sentence point of view.
2. Present focus: AI product work and the 11ai / 11bench / 11io destinations.
3. Selected tools, grouped by practical use rather than logo inventory.
4. Four recent/relevant experience anchors that establish depth in data products, product delivery, developer experience, and leadership.
5. Accurate direct contact paths.

### Put behind a disclosure

- Earlier work: Glaiveware, Sycret.ink, American Heart Association, NextBitt, and Science4you. These are legitimate career context, but the current profile should not make a visitor scan a full chronology before reaching current open work.
- Detailed responsibilities from each employer, which are better served by the CV at cv.rj11.io.

### Omit from the README

- Full home address, phone number, or any contact data not in the PDFs: privacy and source discipline.
- Unverified client names, metrics, stars, follower counts, contribution activity, availability, or endorsements: absent from the PDFs.
- "Self-guided missile" and the longer gaming/clan story: present in the extended CV, but its tone is less useful for a GitHub visitor than the concrete work narrative.
- Education: valid but not needed to establish the profile’s current engineering focus; it remains in the CV.
- Specific claims of proficiency, best-in-class quality, success, or business impact: the PDFs describe responsibilities and technology, not comparable outcomes.
